Effective detection engineering isn't just about collecting logs; it's about connecting the dots. Today we're diving deep into correlation logic that catches lateral movement techniques often missed by standard rule sets.

When analyzing authentication logs, specifically SSH activity, it's crucial to distinguish between brute force attempts and automated script failures.

warning

Critical Warning

Ensure you filter out known localhost traffic before applying this rule to production environments to avoid high-volume false positives.

Use the following one-liner to rapidly parse your auth logs for outlier behavior regarding failed passwords:

bash 
grep -r "failed password" /var/log/auth.log | \
awk {print $11}' | \
sort | uniq -c | \
sort -nr

This pipeline extracts the 11th field (typically the IP address in standard auth log formats), counts unique occurrences, and sorts them numerically in reverse order. This highlights the most aggressive IPs instantly.
